HOW DO I SEE MYSELF 5 YEARS FROM NOW?

When I started college, I didn't expect the challenges that would come my way. I thought it would be similar to high school, where I could just coast through. But some people say that college is the first major stepping stone towards achieving your dreams and becoming who you want to be in the future. It all began when the second semester started at our school. I didn't realize how difficult it would be to pass my subjects, but this is just a part of what you have to go through if you want to succeed.

This brought up thoughts like, "What if I'm not on the right course? What if I don't pass? What if this isn't really for me?" These are the questions that constantly run through my mind. “Maybe if I had pursued the course I wanted, I wouldn't be struggling this much. Or even if I did struggle, at least I'd be doing something I love." But no, I shouldn't think that way. It's not always about what I want. Given the hardships of life, I can't afford to aim for something impossible in my current situation.

So, if I see myself 5 years from now, I hope to be working in an industry that aligns with my course, even though programming isn't my passion. But I know that IT is a vast field with many areas to explore, and I am still finding the one that will excel my skills.

Yet I really picture myself working in a good company, perhaps in a movie production. Where I would be a director and to publish a movie of my own.

And I am committed to being able to provide for my family's needs. And to give back all the sacrifices my mother and father made for me.

I will ensure they will experience the kind of life they never had before. We won't have to worry about our bills or daily meals. And my mother will finally be able to return to her home province after 27 years.

As for myself, I see a woman who continues to pursue the things she has loved since she was a child, who keeps reaching for her goals and ambitions, and who studies the course she truly desires because she is now financially stable.

I hope that all of this will come true. Though only the Lord knows what lies ahead, But I believe that if you work hard, nothing is impossible.

And I always remind myself that sometimes the things you want do not align with God's plans. But you don't realize that He is just preparing you for something better.
